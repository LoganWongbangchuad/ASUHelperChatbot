English creates.

English creates opportunities to develop career skills in communication, multicultural appreciation, critical thinking, technical writing, creative writing, teaching and business writing.

When you major in English, you will learn the problem-solving, research and analytical skills that are useful in any career field. You have the option of further specializing your degree in one of two areas, creative writing or technical and business writing. A teacher certification option is also available. No matter which specialization you choose, you’ll have plenty of opportunities to gain hands-on experience both in and out of the classroom, preparing you for your career upon graduation.

Our graduates work in the U.S. and internationally in technology, government agencies, nonprofit organizations, education, finance and retail, and as creative writers, artists and small business owners.

Why Earn My English Degree at Angelo State?

World-Class Conference Opportunities

The Natalie Zan Ryan Department of English and Modern Languages is home to the annual ASU Writers Conference in Honor of Elmer Kelton. Writers from all over the country join us on campus for an engaging two-day conference, which brings award-winning authors from all genres to share their works and creativity with the ASU and local communities.

Degree-Enhancing Opportunities

Enhance your degree with one of our many minors! Our minors in English, Creative Writing and Technical Writing pair great with most academic majors. Plus, we offer several scholarships specifically for students in our department. You can also gain hands-on experience helping publish our Oasis literary magazine, or as a tutor in our Writing Center.

Networking Opportunities

Our department offers a variety of student organizations to help you get connected with your fellow English and modern languages students and professors. Join one (or several!) to take part in our social activities and develop your professional and networking skills.
