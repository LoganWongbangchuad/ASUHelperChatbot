  1. Complete an application through ApplyTexas.

    You must be admitted to Angelo State to have access to our over 600 scholarship opportunities. If you are admitted after the priority deadline(s), you will be considered for scholarships to the extent funds are available.  

    General Scholarship Priority Deadline: Jan. 15
    Distinguished Scholarship Priority Deadline: April 1

  2. Pay your $50 non-refundable application fee.

    The day after submitting your application, you will receive an email with a link to pay the application fee. You can also pay your application fee at any time through RamPort, our portal. You can set up and access your RamPort account here. Fee waivers are available for qualifying students.

    Please note: we are unable to refund the application fee once it has been paid.

  3. Submit your high school transcript and other required documents.

    High School Transcripts
    Your transcript should include your class rank and diploma type. Please do not submit your transcripts via email. Request your high school send your transcript directly to us through TREx (which we strongly encourage), or they can be sent via mail to:

    ASU Station #11014
    San Angelo, TX 76909

    Other Required Documents
    You may need to submit additional documents. Please view our admission requirements for more information.

  4. Submit your ACT/SAT scores OR indicate you are test optional.

    If you have already taken the ACT or SAT or plan to take either test, we encourage you to submit your scores to us.

    • Submit your ACT/SAT Scores: Sign in to your MyACT or College Board account and request your Official Score Report be sent to ASU.

    • If you would like to download your own copy of your Official Scores Report, you can upload it through our Secure Document Submissions Form. Please be sure that the report you submit does not state they are “Unofficial Scores.”

    If you want to be considered for admission without your test scores, you must indicate this on your application. Learn more about ASU’s test optional policy.

Admission Requirements 

You are guaranteed admission into Angelo State if you are graduating from an accredited high school and meet one of the following qualifications. If you do not meet the qualifications below, your application will be reviewed based on additional criteria.

Next 25%** 2.5 or higher high school GPA OR 17 / 920

4th Quarter Application Review OR Application Review

Dual Credit: Students who have completed at least six hours of ASU dual credit courses with a GPA of 3.0 or higher will receive automatic admission to ASU.

If you are a homeschool student and present evidence of completing a nontraditional secondary education without an official class rank, you will have a class rank assigned by the Office of Admissions. This process is in accordance with Texas Senate Bill 1543. The class rank will be comparable to the average class rank of applicants with equivalent ACT or SAT scores.

After you have been assigned a class rank, you will be admitted if you meet the requirements. All other applications will go through a file review.

Additional Information

Official college transcripts and/or Advanced Placement scores must be submitted in order to receive earned credit. Fall applicants are encouraged to submit official college transcripts in January showing all dual credit coursework completed up to that point. A final college transcript should be submitted following completion of all dual credit coursework and prior to advising.

Request an official transcript directly from the college/university providing the college credit. Visit our transcript resources page for links for how to order transcripts from other Texas colleges and universities.

If you submit ACT or SAT scores, AP test scores or additional scores for admission purposes, you may be eligible for credit.

The Texas Success Initiative (TSI) helps ensure you’re ready for college-level classes and you possess the necessary academic skills to succeed. Please ensure we have your TSI documentation on record.

Your residency classification is initially based on the information provided on your ApplyTexas application.
